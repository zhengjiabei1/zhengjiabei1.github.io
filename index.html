About

I am a senior undergraduate student at Beijing Forestry University, majoring in Geographic Information Science with a Micro-degree in Artificial Intelligence & Its Applications (GPA 88.17/100). My work sits at the intersection of AI and scientific data — I build data pipelines that transform raw, heterogeneous scientific data into AI-ready training sets, and develop retrieval-augmented systems for scientific knowledge discovery.

At AI for Science Institute (AISI), I evaluate LLMs on scientific QA benchmarks, construct multi-modal scientific training corpora, and experiment with hybrid vector-database retrieval using LangChain. Previously, I have processed satellite imagery for wetland vegetation mapping at the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and automated large-scale data pipelines in industry. I enjoy working on end-to-end ML projects — from messy real-world data to deployed, interpretable models.
