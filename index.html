About

I am a senior undergraduate student at Beijing Forestry University, majoring in Geographic Information Science with a Micro-degree in Artificial Intelligence and Its Applications (GPA 88.17/100). My work sits at the intersection of AI and scientific data — I build data pipelines that transform raw, heterogeneous scientific data into AI-ready training sets, and develop retrieval-augmented systems for scientific knowledge discovery.

I am currently an intern at the AI for Science Institute (AISI), where I construct multi-modal scientific training corpora, evaluate LLMs on scientific QA benchmarks, and build hybrid vector-database retrieval systems with LangChain. Previously, I processed satellite imagery for wetland vegetation mapping at the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and automated large-scale data pipelines in industry.
